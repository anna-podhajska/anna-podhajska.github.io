What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

Compassion along with ambition and humility are features of great leaders. I worked both with compassionate and not compassionate leaders in Poland and Ireland. Compassion has very real business benefits that result in stronger relationships, engagement and as a result trust and better work quality.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

The idea seems similar to one of the aspects of behaviourism where the new habit is a result of brain creating new connections when action and reaction are repeated long enough. All this may simply be called different ways of learning. I find the multiplying goodness meditation strengthening compassion.

Do the Tonglen meditation. How did you find it?

“Tonglen" means “giving and receiving in Tibetan. Since our brains respond far more strongly to negative experiences than comparable positive ones, this practice seems powerful and useful however difficult for novice.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

From my experience, those are the conversations that we unconsciously avoid and prepare for them better than usually. I believe that being calm, open-minded, focused on solving the problem and compassionate helps handling the conversation, and the conversation result may be very satisfying.
